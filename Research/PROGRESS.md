This is remarkably close to firing 16's 143-byte figure for the 3rd→4th type transition — a 3-byte, ~2% difference between two consecutive marginal-cost measurements. Two consistent data points is real evidence (not proof, but real evidence) that the marginal cost per additional `signal<>`-backed event type (this specific shape: free-function listener, default capacity) is genuinely flat at roughly **~144-145 bytes**, not drifting up or down across the 3rd/4th/5th-type range — directly answering the question this firing was scoped to answer.
  **A genuinely unexpected secondary finding while reading the `nm` output**: `lweh::signal<wifi_rssi_event,4>::publish()` and `lweh::signal<battery_level_event,4>::publish()` sit at the **exact same address** (`400800d0`, both 0x18=24 bytes) in the linked binary — the linker folded them into one shared copy. This makes sense on reflection (`publish()`'s loop body only touches `Event` through a `const&` parameter passed straight into `delegate::operator()`, so its machine code can come out byte-identical across sufficiently-similar `Event` types even without any deliberate type erasure), but it was not something anyone had confirmed empirically before this firing. By contrast, the `free_function_stub<&on_battery_level>` and `free_function_stub<&on_wifi_rssi>` trampolines did **not** fold (distinct addresses, `40080054` vs `40080070`) — correctly so, since each one's `template<auto Fn>` NTTP bakes in a genuinely different call target. **This likely explains part of why the measured marginal cost (143-146 bytes) already undercuts research.md §A10's a-priori "naive, no shared core" estimate of 150-400 bytes/type** — the linker (under `-flto`, per `lw_eh_size_flags`) is apparently already recovering some sharing on its own for the parts of `signal<>` that happen to compile identically, without needing the deliberately-deferred `detail::signal_core` extraction to force it. Not yet confirmed which specific mechanism is responsible (IPA-ICF vs. plain COMDAT section merging vs. something else in this GCC 8.4.0/binutils combination) — flagging for a future codegen-deep-verification firing rather than asserting a mechanism I haven't actually looked at in disassembly.
