Ruled out the repository itself as a cause (`.git` is a healthy 966KB, 281 loose objects, zero packs — nothing that would justify a 500MB allocation for pushing a few KB of diff; the fixed 500MB figure recurring across every failure is almost certainly a fixed-size buffer git's HTTP/pack transfer layer allocates regardless of actual payload size, not something proportional to what's being pushed). Tried and ruled out reduced-memory git packing settings (`pack.threads=1`, `pack.window=1`) as ineffective across firings 21 and 25's attempts. ~13 total push attempts across firings 21/24/25/26/27 combined; firings 21 and 24 each resolved after exactly one retry at the start of the next firing, but firings 25→26→27 have not, indicating the underlying condition has become more chronic than the earlier transient pattern.

GitHub's own Actions parser tolerates the bare form in practice (countless real-world workflows use it), but quoting (`"on":`) is unambiguous under any YAML parser and costs nothing, so there was no reason to rely on that tolerance once the ambiguity was noticed. Fixed and amended into the still-unpublished commit (safe: it has never reached `origin/main`, so amending doesn't rewrite any history anyone else could have seen or based work on — a meaningfully different situation than amending a pushed commit, which this loop has correctly never done).
